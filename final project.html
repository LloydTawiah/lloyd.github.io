Democratic Leadership
It is a type of leadership whereby the leader accepts the views of his followers, Power is vested in the leader as well as the followers, he suggests during decision making. Advantages of democratic leadership It enhances co-operation or teamwork: This is because the leader takes into consideration the ideas decisions, or choices of his or her followers. It causes the followers to feel respected and accepted in the group. There is a high motivation: This is as a result of the respect felt by the followers. It causes them to put in their best for the success of the entire group. There is high initiate: This is a situation whereby the goals or objectives of the group are implemented or achieved of high motivation experienced by the group members.

Fast forward, in the NT the four gospel gives different account of the resurrection of Christ. The four authors of the gospel wrote the account of the life of Jesus with different aims in mind. Matthew wrote his account to a largely Jewish audience to convince them that Jesus Christ was the messiah prophesied in their scriptures. Mark wrote to a largely Roma audience to explain that Jesus was the obedient servant of God. Luke, the gentile himself wrote to a largely Greek audience to show that Jesus was the perfect God –man. John wrote to the Church at large to both Jew and Gentile to show that Jesus Christ was the divine son of God John 20: 30-31. The three gospel (Matthew, Mark And John) are called synoptic gospel because these three gospels display a high degree of similarity of content, narrative, arrangement, language and sentence and paragraph structures.
